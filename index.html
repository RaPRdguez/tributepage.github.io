Ernest Rutherford

-- The Physicist with a Chemistry Nobel Prize --

Ernest Rutherford made incredible achievements through his long life. Here's is a time line of this genius' discoveries and distintions:

  • 30 August 1871 Rutherford was born at Brightwater, near Nelson, New Zealand.
  • 1899 Ernest Rutherford discovered rays from radioactive materials, which he named Alpha and Beta.
  • 1899 Discovered with Robert B. Owens the gas element Radon.
  • 1908 It is awarded with the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his investigations into the disintegration of the elements and the chemistry of radioactive substances.
  • 1911 Formulates the known as Rutherford Model of the Atom.
  • 1919 Rutherford artificially provoked a nuclear reaction in a stable element.
  • 1920 Postulated the hydrogen nucleus to be a new particle, the proton.
  • 19 October 1937 Ernest Rutherford died in Cambridge following a short illness and was buried in Westminster Abbey.
